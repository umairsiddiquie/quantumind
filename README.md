## 📌 Overview

**Quantumind** is a groundbreaking research initiative and cognitive architecture model that proposes a novel framework for understanding human cognition by integrating principles from quantum information theory — specifically **weak measurement, quantum coherence, and ethical entanglement** — into the study of decision-making, memory, and moral reasoning.

This project bridges speculative philosophy with empirical science, offering a new paradigm for understanding the mind as a quantum-adaptive system. The core hypothesis is that human cognition exhibits measurable **quantum-like behavior**, including non-classical probability patterns, context-dependent memory states, and entangled ethical values.

The model is validated through a combination of **EEG, fMRI, and behavioral experiments**, and is presented with a mathematical formalism for reproducible analysis. The implications extend to **AI alignment, cognitive resilience, and ethical design in intelligent systems**.

## 🧭 Core Concepts

### 1. **Weak Measurement in Cognition**
   - Human decisions are not always deterministic; they are influenced by *partial observations* — akin to weak measurements in quantum mechanics.
   - Demonstrated through probabilistic choice experiments under uncertainty.

### 2. **Quantum Coherence in Memory**
   - Memory states are not static; they exist in superposition until “collapsed” by context.
   - Validated via fMRI data showing neural coherence patterns during recall.

### 3. **Ethical Entanglement**
   - Moral values are not independent; they are *entangled* — meaning a decision on one ethical dimension influences another.
   - Measured through multi-dimensional moral dilemmas and correlated neural responses.

### 4. **Quantum Probability Framework**
   - A mathematical model based on quantum probability theory (e.g., Born rule, Hilbert space representation) to predict human behavior.
   - Implemented in Python for simulation and validation.
