### 📌 Overview

**Quantumind** is a **multidisciplinary research initiative, open-source project, and scientific hypothesis** exploring whether human cognition — particularly in moral reasoning, memory, and decision-making under uncertainty — exhibits measurable quantum-like behavior. It is not a fictional concept, but a **testable, falsifiable, and publishable scientific model** grounded in quantum information theory, cognitive science, and AI ethics.

This repository serves as the **central hub** for:

- 🧪 **Experimental protocols** to test quantum cognition (weak measurement, entanglement, decoherence resistance)
- 📊 **Open-access data, code, and visualizations** (all under CC-BY 4.0)
- 📄 **Scientific papers, preprints, and Zenodo metadata** for peer-reviewed publication
- 🎨 **Visual design assets** — including the iconic “glowing brain” neural network — for branding and public engagement
- 📈 **Grant templates, pitch decks, and funding proposals** for collaborators and investors
- 🧭 **Theoretical frameworks** — including the “Chronal Ruler” phase-space model of time, and “Weak Measurement Listening” as a cognitive protocol

---

### 🧬 Core Hypothesis

> Human cognition exhibits quantum-like behavior — including superposition of options, weak measurement of fragile states, and entangled ethical values — which can be modeled, optimized, and applied to ethical AI alignment and cognitive resilience.

---

### 🧩 Key Components

- **QuantumMind Architecture** — A cognitive model using quantum probability, coherence, and entanglement.
- **Weak Measurement Protocol** — To study how memory and moral stances change under non-invasive observation.
- **Ethical Entanglement Framework** — To model how moral values correlate with context.
- **Decoherence Resistance Metrics** — To measure cognitive stability under noise or misinformation.
- **Open-Source Tools** — Python code, Jupyter notebooks, and simulation environments for replication.
